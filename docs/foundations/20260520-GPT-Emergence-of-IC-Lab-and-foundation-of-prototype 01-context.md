# 20260520 — Emergence of IC-Lab and Foundation of Prototype 01

## Context

A structural observation emerged during work on **Boost Hyper Engine (BHE)**.

BHE is proving to be a naturally long-term project. This is not a problem nor a delay: it is coherent with its nature.

Its objectives — pedagogical abstraction, modularity, reusable interactions, renderer separation, evaluation logic, accessibility and long-term maintainability — require time, iterations, comparisons, hesitations and progressive emergence.

Trying to accelerate this process artificially would likely damage the quality of architectural decisions.

At the same time, another reality exists:

* teachers need to see concrete things;
* pedagogical hypotheses benefit from being materialised quickly;
* discussions with researchers and practitioners become richer when supported by demonstrators;
* the REPLI4C context creates a need for fast experimentation around intercomprehension.

A tension therefore appeared between two temporalities:

```txt id="2bnv7g"
BHE → deep, slow, architectural time

Field experimentation → fast, concrete, discussable time
```

The idea of **IC-Lab** emerged as a response to this tension.

---

## Birth of IC-Lab

The idea emerged during a break between working sessions.

Rather than trying to accelerate BHE or lower its ambitions, the solution became:

> create a parallel experimental space.

**IC-Lab (Intercomprehension Lab)** is conceived as a lightweight prototyping laboratory dedicated to rapid experimentation around intercomprehension.

Its purpose is not to replace BHE.

Instead:

```txt id="k3e6au"
BHE = deep pedagogical architecture

IC-Lab = rapid pedagogical experimentation
```

The relationship is complementary.

IC-Lab can produce signals, observations, interaction ideas and pedagogical intuitions that may later nourish BHE.

BHE, in return, remains the deeper long-term horizon.

---

## Philosophy of the laboratory

Several working principles emerged immediately.

### Priorities

IC-Lab privileges:

* rapid demonstrators;
* pedagogical observability;
* lightweight and modifiable code;
* small iteration cycles;
* concrete discussion material for teachers and researchers;
* experimentation before systematisation.

### What IC-Lab intentionally avoids

IC-Lab avoids:

* premature frameworks;
* overengineering;
* excessive abstraction;
* false promises of finished products;
* technological complexity without pedagogical value.

A prototype may remain imperfect.

Its main purpose is to:

> make something visible, discussable and observable.

The working principle adopted today became:

> **Prototype rapide. Observation sérieuse. Complexité différée.**

---

## Prototype 01 — Seven Sieves Explorer

The first prototype of IC-Lab became:

> **Prototype 01 — Seven Sieves Explorer**

Its purpose is to explore whether guided observation through the **seven sieves of intercomprehension** can support meaning construction in an unfamiliar text.

The prototype intentionally privileges observation over correction and inference over translation.

### Implemented sieves

The following seven sieves were progressively implemented during the same session:

1. **Lexique international**
2. **Lexique pan-roman**
3. **Correspondances phonétiques**
4. **Graphies / prononciations**
5. **Syntaxe pan-romane (simplified)**
6. **Morphosyntaxe légère**
7. **Préfixes / suffixes**

### Core interaction

A strong interaction pattern emerged:

> **word inspection by click**

Instead of correcting answers or imposing explanations, the prototype allows learners to inspect words and progressively observe signals through different interpretive layers.

The system intentionally uses cautious formulations:

* probable
* possible
* often
* signal
* observation

This decision avoids the illusion of certainty and better supports exploratory reading.

### Deliberate simplifications

Several temptations were intentionally resisted:

* no linguistic engine;
* no automatic parser;
* no large lexical database;
* no complete grammatical analysis;
* no translation system;
* no AI-supported interpretation.

The prototype remains:

> lightweight, pedagogically oriented and rapidly modifiable.

---

## First emerging signals

Several promising observations already emerged during construction.

### Layered reading appears promising

The sieve logic functions well as overlapping interpretive layers.

Different sieves invite different forms of reading:

* immediate recognition;
* lexical family recognition;
* phonetic reconstruction;
* grapho-phonetic caution;
* syntactic intuition;
* grammatical feeling;
* morphological clues.

### Word inspection appears central

The click interaction increasingly appears to be the core of the experience.

It transforms reading from:

> “trying to decode everything”

into:

> “progressively observing useful signals”.

### Humble explanations feel pedagogically stronger

Unexpectedly, deliberately modest explanations (“possible”, “probable”, “may suggest”) felt stronger than authoritative correction.

This may align well with intercomprehension philosophy.

### Fast prototyping proved highly productive

A complete, conceptually coherent prototype was produced rapidly through iterative collaboration between human reflection and Codex implementation.

This strongly reinforces the value of IC-Lab as a complementary research and experimentation strategy.

---

## What is intentionally postponed

Prototype 01 intentionally postpones:

* multi-language expansion;
* learner persistence;
* adaptive progression;
* complete corpora;
* linguistic robustness;
* deep grammatical analysis;
* broader lexical coverage;
* research protocol instrumentation.

These elements may emerge later if pedagogically justified.

---

## Immediate next steps

Short term:

* freeze Prototype 01 as a coherent demonstrator;
* document observations;
* delay external teacher testing until the prototype stabilises conceptually;
* discuss Prototype 02.

One possible direction already identified:

> **Language Shift**

A progressive movement across Romance languages designed to create the experience:

> “Wait… I still understand.”

## A final methodological note

An unexpected but important observation emerged today:

the productive role of pauses.

The idea of IC-Lab did not emerge during maximal concentration, but during a break between working periods.

This reinforces a practical intuition:

> reflection, decantation and cognitive recombination are not interruptions of production.

They are part of production itself.
