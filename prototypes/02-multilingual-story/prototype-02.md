# Prototype 02 — Multilingual Story

## Intent

This prototype explores whether a progressively multilingual narrative can support meaning construction despite language shifts.

The goal is not translation.

The goal is to explore:

> **confidence in understanding despite partial comprehension.**

The prototype seeks to observe whether a reader continues building meaning while the language gradually changes.

Instead of asking:

> “Did you understand correctly?”

the prototype asks:

> “Did you continue understanding?”

---

## Core pedagogical hypothesis

A learner may understand more than expected when:

* context remains stable;
* narrative continuity is preserved;
* languages change progressively rather than abruptly;
* comprehension relies on inference rather than translation.

The prototype aims to produce an experience of discovery:

> “Wait… I still understand.”

---

## Prototype logic

The experience is based on four interacting principles:

```txt
progressive narrative
+
gentle multilingual shift
+
micro-observations
+
soft metacognition
```

The reader is not tested.

The reader is accompanied.

The experience should feel exploratory rather than evaluative.

---

## User experience principles

### Progressive unfolding

The story must never appear all at once.

The experience unfolds:

* scene by scene;
* sentence by sentence;
* with small pauses and breathing moments.

The rhythm should feel calm.

Almost conversational.

The prototype intentionally slows reading to reduce linguistic anxiety.

---

### No punitive correction

No score.

No failure.

No “correct answer”.

Questions exist to observe perception and strategies.

The prototype should encourage:

> curiosity over performance.

---

### Soft observation

Micro-questions appear progressively during the story.

Examples:

#### Perceived comprehension

```txt
🙂 I understand well

😐 I think I understand

😵 I feel lost
```

#### Strategy identification

```txt
What helped you here?

□ a familiar word
□ context
□ story continuity
□ guessing
□ I don't know
```

#### Prediction

```txt
What do you think will happen next?
```

#### Metacognitive reflection

```txt
Did you notice something changing?
```

---

## What we want to observe

The prototype seeks to explore several dimensions.

### Perceived comprehension

Does the learner feel they continue understanding?

### Moment of uncertainty

When does confidence decrease?

### Strategies used

What supports meaning construction?

* cognates?
* context?
* repetition?
* narrative logic?

### Tolerance to incomprehension

Does the learner continue despite uncertainty?

Or stop?

### Narrative engagement

Does curiosity sustain comprehension effort?

---

## Narrative packs

The underlying mechanism remains stable.

Only the narrative universe changes.

### Pack A — Lumi and the mysterious box

**Target audience**

* primary school
* FLE learners
* discovery of IC
* general public

**Tone**

Warm, playful, reassuring.

A little adventurous.

A little afraid.

But always curious.

#### Character

🐭 **Lumi**

Lumi is:

* curious;
* slightly clumsy;
* observant;
* a traveller;
* a little afraid sometimes;

but:

> **she moves forward anyway.**

Lumi functions as a subtle metaphor of the learner in intercomprehension.

She does not understand everything.

She observes.

She guesses.

She hesitates.

She continues.

#### First story

**Lumi and the mysterious box**

Lumi explores an old attic.

She discovers a strange little box.

Small clues written in different languages progressively appear.

The reader crosses languages together with Lumi.

Without fully noticing at first.

The emotional objective is:

> trust despite partial understanding.

---

### Pack B — The forgotten notebook

**Target audience**

* university students
* adult learners
* teachers
* researchers

**Tone**

Reflective.

Curious.

Light intellectual mystery.

#### Premise

A traveller, researcher or student discovers an old notebook.

Its annotations progressively shift between Romance languages.

The reader slowly realises:

> understanding continues despite language changes.
